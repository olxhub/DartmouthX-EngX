Explore: Beams Part II

Let continue to experiment with the Beam Simulator. Now you get to choose the material and cross-sectional shape in addition to the load, span and type of beam; as you vary these different options pay attention to the size of the beam required to support the load. Can you minimize the size and area of the beam cross-section that is required, while maximizing the load the may be supported?

Let’s again start with a simply-supported beam with a span of 10m that supports a distributed load of 50kN/m. We found earlier that the maximum shear was at the supports, while the maximum bending moment was at the middle of the beam. 

After answering the 2 Finding questions, explore more questions such as:

  1. Select a material type and cross-sectional shape and size and determine the size of the cross-section that is required.
  2. Try changing the material type. What happens to the size of the required cross-section?
  3. Try changing the cross-sectional shape. How does the shape affect the size of the cross-section?
  4. Have fun experimenting! What is the most load your beam is able to support? Share your loading value and design with the class through the discussion board.
Using the Simulation  Expand ↓
  • Click and drag the circles to slide the values of Length, Load, Load Point, and Support.
  • Note, the color of the load arrows is not relevant and is not color-coded to tension or compression.
  • Use the 'Loading' drop-down menu to choose a Concentrated load or Distributed load.
  • Use the drop-down menus on the right side to choose a Material and Beam Cross-Section
  • Shear and Moment values are shown on the left side. Also watch the visualization of the shear and moment diagrams.
