Explore: Beams Part I

Use the beam simulator to experiment with shear and bending.  Have fun!

Here's a starting point for you: Design a simply-supported beam with a span of 10m that supports a distributed load of 50kN/m.  Answer the first 3 Finding questions found under the simulation. These will give you a good base to build from.

After answering the 3 Finding questions, explore more questions such as:

  1. What happens if you increase the span of the beam?
  2. What happens if you increase the magnitude of the load? 
  3. What happens the the shear forces and moments if you change the material? or the cross-section?
  4. How does the response change if you use a propped beam or a cantilever beam instead of a simply-supported beam? Where is the shear the maximum for each type of beam? Where is the bending moment the maximum for each type of beam?

Using the Simulation  Expand ↓
  • Click and drag the circles to slide the values of Length, Load, Load Point, and Support.
  • Note, the color of the load arrows is not relevant and is not color-coded to tension or compression.
  • Use the 'Loading' drop-down menu to choose a Concentrated load or Distributed load.
  • Shear and Moment values are shown on the left side. Also watch the visualization of the shear and moment diagrams.
